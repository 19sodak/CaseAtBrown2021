what is Case@Brown?

Get ready for two days of competition, workshops, and networking!

At Case@Brown, you’ll join your classmates in participating in the first and only student-run case competition on Brown’s campus, in which teams of four will compete to develop the best solution to a business-related case study provided by Altman Vilandrie. The case will be about the cloud gaming and video game streaming market, an up-and-coming space in technology! Again, no prior knowledge at all is required - additional data and information will be provided in both the keynote presentation and the slides that are distributed for the competition itself.

You’ll have the opportunity to present in front of professionals, listen to talks by representatives from firms like EY-Parthenon, and meet other students who are interested in management consulting - those who are not competing are still welcome to attend the workshops and networking sessions! Winners of the case competition will have the unique chance to attend an exclusive networking dinner with Altman Vilandrie, a top strategy consulting firm.

Whether you’ve already gone through the recruiting process or you’re just starting to explore consulting, Case@Brown welcomes individuals of all backgrounds (no experience necessary)!

If you have any other questions, check our our FAQ or feel free to reach out to Sheri, our case competition chair.

faq

Who can participate in Case@Brown?
The competition is open to all Brown students.

What are the prizes?
The main prize of the competition will be an exclusive networking dinner for the winning team with the gold sponsor. This will serve as an excellent opportunity to get to know the firm on a more intimate level, and the experience overall will be a great addition to any resume.

Is any experience in consulting necessary to participate?
Not at all! Our main focus for Case@Brown is for it to be a learning experience for you, the participant. There will be many workshops open to students to learn about the nature of consulting and to get started with their cases!

Will BCC match me with a team if I do not have a team?
Yes, BCC will match you up with team members based on your preferences as stated in the application form if you do not have your own team.

Is there an application process to participate?
There is an application to participate in the case competition which will be released at the end of January. For the workshops and networking event, you must fill out a Google Form to indicate that you will be attending in order for us to track attendance, as there will be limited spots available.

Is there a fee to participate?
None!

How many members should each team have for the case competition?
4 members per team.

What is the format of the competition?
Teams will be asked to solve a case problem (Ex: Should Company X acquire Company Y?). At the end, company representatives judge the solutions presented by each team and the top 5 teams will be invited to present their solutions.

Can I go to the workshops and networking event if I do not join the competiton?
Yes!
